Southrepps Church


This massive church rides the gentle hills to the south of Cromer. Its great tower is visible for miles, one of the tallest in the county. Until the late 18th century, the huge nave had aisles as well. It must have been one of the biggest churches in England.
Source: http://www.norfolkchurches.co.uk/southrepps/southrepps.htm
Southrepps War Memorial


This memorial commemorates the residents of Southrepps who were killed or missing in the First World War (20 names) and the Second World War (3 names)
Source: https://www.tracesofwar.com/sights/87244/War-Memorial-Southrepps.htm
Southrepps Wildlife Reserve


Set in mainly agricultural land on the south west edge of Lower Street in Southrepps, this site of 3.4 hectares, comprises a mixture of mixed native woodland and scrub growing on an undulating mosaic of dryer and wetter areas.
Source: https://www.norfolkwildlifetrust.org.uk/wildlife-in-norfolk/county-wildlife-sites/school-common-,-southrepps-(1194)
